📌 Project Overview

This project focuses on analyzing large-scale e-commerce sales, customer behavior, and marketing campaign performance using Snowflake (SQL-based analytics) and Power BI.

The objective was to identify revenue growth challenges, high-value customer segments, marketing ROI performance, and product demand trends to support data-driven business decisions.

Dataset Size:
• 1.5 Million Order Records  
• 300,000 Customer Profiles  
• 500,000 Marketing Campaign Records  

---

🛠 Tech Stack

Snowflake  
Advanced SQL  
Power BI  
Excel (data validation support)

---

🗂 Database Schema

The database consists of 3 core analytical tables:

Orders  
Customers  
Marketing Campaigns  

Implemented:

• Primary Keys  
• Data Relationships (Customer ↔ Orders)  
• Derived Analytical Fields  
• Revenue Calculation  
• Customer Segmentation Fields  

---

🧹 Data Cleaning & Feature Engineering

Performed structured data preparation including:

• Revenue calculation (price × quantity)  
• Customer age group segmentation  
• Customer churn flag creation using purchase history  
• Data validation across transactional and marketing datasets  
• Verification of unique customer and order records  

These steps ensured the dataset was ready for reliable business analysis.

---

📊 Business Analysis Performed

1️⃣ Sales Performance Analysis

• Monthly revenue trends  
• Product category revenue contribution  
• Regional sales distribution  
• Payment method usage analysis  
• Top selling product identification  

---

2️⃣ Profitability Insights

• Average order value by product category  
• Revenue performance comparison across regions  

---

3️⃣ Customer Behavior Analysis

• Customer churn rate analysis  
• Revenue contribution by age group  
• High-value customer identification (Top 5% spenders)  
• Customer value analysis by city tier  

---

4️⃣ Marketing Performance Analysis

• Conversion rate analysis by marketing channel  
• Marketing ROI calculation  
• Regional marketing performance comparison  

---

📈 Key Insights

• Customers aged **19–30** generated the highest share of revenue  
• **Tier 1 cities** showed significantly higher customer value  
• A small percentage of high-spending customers contributed a large portion of revenue  
• Marketing channels showed large variation in **conversion rates and ROI**  
• Certain product categories consistently generated higher revenue
